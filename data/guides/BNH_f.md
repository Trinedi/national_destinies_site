## Concept

Beth Nahrain (Syriac for "between rivers", the medieval self-designation
of Mesopotamia) is the Tier 1 stage of a two-step Syriac restoration
chain. It represents the medieval Syriac Christian polity that almost
formed: Tur Abdin maintained de facto autonomy under successive
overlords into the 14th century, the Patriarchate of the East at
Seleucia-Ctesiphon retained quasi-political authority over a vast
Christian diaspora, and the County of Edessa (1098 to 1144) was a real
Crusader-Syriac hybrid state. BNH consolidates these threads into a
formal Syriac Christian duchy ruling upper Mesopotamia.

Forming BNH is a survival and restoration story, not an imperial one.
The formation event commits you to one of three doctrines:
**Patriarchal Restoration** (clergy-led patriarchate state),
**Tur Abdin Defenders** (monastic-warrior highland kingdom), or
**Edessene Renaissance** (Latin-Syriac cosmopolitan hybrid). Each
sets a 25-year timed modifier and a destiny path variable.

BNH is **Tier 1 of a chain.** Tier 2 is the L4 Assyrian Empire (NIN),
which gates on having previously formed BNH. Forming BNH is the
foundation step, not the destination.

## Why no auto-recommendation

The formation gate requires miaphysite or nestorianism religion. No
country starts as nestorianism, so the auto-derived list of nestorian
candidates is empty. The miaphysite list is mostly Ethiopian or Nubian
tags that are geographically too distant from the Mesopotamian
heartland. The Caucasian Armenian principalities and ATZ are the
practical paths, and they need calling out by hand.

Cilicia (CIL) is the biggest miss from this list. It is miaphysite and
geographically adjacent to the Edessa zone, but it starts at Kingdom
rank, which makes it ineligible for the Duchy-tier BNH formable. CIL
players should target Tier 2 NIN directly instead, which has a parallel
kingdom-rank path.

## Forming it

1. **Pick a small Caucasian Armenian principality** (Khachen, Syunik,
   Amberd, Vayots Dzor) or **ATZ** for the geographically simplest
   path. Avoid Cilicia, Greater Armenia, Ethiopia, and any other tag
   that starts above County rank.
2. **Survive the early Mongol-successor pressure.** Most candidate
   tags begin as Ilkhanid vassals or under similar overlordship.
   Either ride out the Ilkhanate collapse or escape vassalage early.
3. **Conquer the three required locations.** Mosul (Nineveh), Nusaybin
   (Tur Abdin core), and Urfa (Edessa). Mosul is the hardest, usually
   under Iraqi or Mongol-successor control. Nusaybin sits in the same
   neighborhood. Urfa is in the contested Edessa-Cilicia frontier zone.
4. **Hold 40 percent of the wider Jazira area.** The fraction
   requirement is loose because the three named cities already gate
   most of the relevant territory.
5. **Pick your formation path.** Patriarch's Restoration leans into
   clergy power, conversion speed, and cultural tradition. Tur Abdin
   Defenders is the militarist option (garrison, manpower, attrition
   pressure on invaders). Edessene Renaissance is the diplomat-trader
   option (diplomatic capacity, trade efficiency, prestige).

## What happens on formation

- **Capital moves to Mosul (Nineveh).**
- **Country culture flips to Syriac.** State pops of Armenian, Iraqi,
  and Kurdish culture also convert to Syriac.
- **Country rank set to Duchy** if you were below it. This is what
  makes the Tier 2 NIN formable reachable next, since NIN at L4 needs
  a current rank below Empire.
- **Stability bonus.**
- **Formation event fires** with the three doctrine choices.

## Notes

- The destiny tree splits two ways: **Path A "Restoration of Aššur"**
  (gates from the Tur Abdin Defenders formation choice, signposts
  toward forming Tier 2 NIN) and **Path B "Light of the East"** (gates
  from the Patriarchal Restoration formation choice, a complete
  civilizational endgame as the Christian-Syriac intermediary between
  Latin west and the Far East).
- The Edessene Renaissance formation choice has no destiny continuation
  by design. Pick it only if you want the formation modifier without
  pursuing either destiny.
- BNH is the first intentional formable to formable chain in the mod.
  Forming BNH unlocks NIN as a downstream goal, but BNH itself is also
  a complete formable with its own heritage tree, advances, custom
  buildings (Catholicate Palace, Tur Abdin Monastery, Bar Hebraeus
  Library, Jundishapur Medical School, others), and destiny capstones
  (Ziggurat of Aššur for Path A, Beth Hekma for Path B).
