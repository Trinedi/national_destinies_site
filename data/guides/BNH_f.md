## Concept

Beth Nahrain is the Syriac name for "between the rivers", the medieval
self-designation of Mesopotamia. The formable represents the medieval
Syriac Christian polity that almost formed: Tur Abdin maintained de
facto autonomy under successive overlords into the 14th century, the
Patriarchate of the East at Seleucia-Ctesiphon retained quasi-political
authority over a vast Christian diaspora, and the County of Edessa
(1098 to 1144) was a real Crusader-Syriac hybrid state. BNH consolidates
these threads into a formal Syriac Christian duchy ruling upper
Mesopotamia.

Forming BNH is a survival and restoration story, not an imperial one.
The imperial counterfactual continues in the Tier 2 formable, the
Assyrian Empire (NIN), which can only be formed after BNH.

## Forming it

1. **Start as one of the priority tags above.** All of them are
   miaphysite Armenian principalities, which is what BNH's potential
   gate requires (miaphysite or nestorianism religion).
2. **Survive the early Mongol-successor pressure.** Most candidate
   tags begin as Ilkhanid vassals or under similar overlordship.
   Either ride out the Ilkhanate collapse or escape vassalage early.
3. **Conquer the three required locations.** Mosul (Nineveh),
   Nusaybin (Tur Abdin core), and Urfa (Edessa). Mosul is the hardest,
   usually under Iraqi or Mongol-successor control. Nusaybin sits in
   the same neighbourhood. Urfa is in the contested Edessa-Cilicia
   frontier zone.
4. **Hold 40 percent of the wider Jazira area.** The fraction
   requirement is loose because the three named cities already gate
   most of the relevant territory.
5. **Pick your formation doctrine.** Three options, each a 25-year
   timed modifier:
   - **Patriarchal Restoration.** Clergy-led patriarchate state.
     Boosts clergy power, conversion speed, and cultural tradition.
   - **Tur Abdin Defenders.** Monastic-warrior highland kingdom.
     Boosts garrison, manpower, and attrition pressure on invaders.
   - **Edessene Renaissance.** Latin-Syriac cosmopolitan hybrid.
     Boosts diplomatic capacity, trade efficiency, and prestige.

## What happens on formation

- Capital moves to Mosul (Nineveh).
- Country culture flips to Syriac. State pops of Armenian, Iraqi, and
  Kurdish culture also convert to Syriac.
- Country rank rises to Duchy if you were below it.
- Stability bonus.
- Formation event fires with the three doctrine choices.

## After forming

The destiny tree splits two ways:

- **Restoration of Aššur.** Unlocked by the Tur Abdin Defenders
  formation choice. A military buildup that signposts toward forming
  the Tier 2 Assyrian Empire (NIN). Capstone building is the Ziggurat
  of Aššur.
- **Light of the East.** Unlocked by the Patriarchal Restoration
  formation choice. A complete civilizational endgame as the Christian
  Syriac intermediary between the Latin west and the Far East. Capstone
  building is Beth Hekma (House of Wisdom).

The Edessene Renaissance formation choice has no destiny continuation.
Pick it only if you want the formation modifier without committing to
either destiny path.
