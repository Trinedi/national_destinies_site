## Concept

Finland in this mod is the frontier resilience archetype: endless
forests, frozen lakes, the sisu that lets a population hardened by
brutal winters outlast invaders that should have crushed it. The
historical references span centuries, from Mikael Agricola codifying
written Finnish in the sixteenth century through the Great Wrath of
the Northern War to the nineteenth-century Finnish national awakening.

The formation question is whether Finland leans into its land
(impenetrable wilderness, shield of the north, Karelian frontier) or
its trade (Ostrobothnian tar, Baltic timber, mercantile connections to
the wider world).

## Forming it

In 1337 Finland is not a free country. The whole `finland_area` (Abo,
Tavastehus, Helsinge, Hollola, Korsholm, Bjorneborg, Vyborg, etc.) is
Swedish territory. The vanilla allow block is empty, so the only gates
are Finnish primary culture and 50 percent of the Finland area.

Two routes are realistic:

1. **Release and play Finland from Sweden.** SWE can release a vassal
   based on the Finnish-cultured cores; that vassal is your start.
   Build up, secure 50 percent of the area, and form FIN.
2. **Break off Finland by rebellion or war.** A separatist revolt under
   Swedish weakness, or a Novgorodian/Russian conquest that frees the
   region into a Finnish-cultured rebel state, can produce a forming
   candidate. This is more opportunistic.

## Pick your founding doctrine on formation

The formation event offers two 15-year doctrines:

- **We are the land itself.** Frontier resilience: the forests and
  lakes as a defensive weapon. Unlocks Path A (Karelian March, Shield
  of the North, Unconquerable Finland).
- **Wealth flows through our rivers and forests.** Tar, timber, fur,
  and the Baltic trade. Unlocks Path B (Ostrobothnian Wealth, Baltic
  Tar Kings, Northern Commercial Empire).

## What happens on formation

- A 15-year formation modifier from your chosen path.
- Mild stability bonus.
- The destiny tree unlocks, plus Mikael Agricola, Great Wrath, and
  national awakening as advance-triggered events later in the run.

## After forming

The destiny tree splits two ways:

- **Path A (Unconquerable Finland).** Karelian March, Shield of the
  North, Unconquerable Finland capstone. Winter Doctrine or Invincible
  Frontier as the twenty-year endgame modifier. The historical answer
  to a Russian or Swedish invasion.
- **Path B (Northern Commercial Empire).** Ostrobothnian Wealth, Baltic
  Tar Kings, Northern Commercial Empire capstone. Mercantile Dominion
  or Industrial Pioneer as the endgame modifier. The Hanseatic-coded
  alternative: turn the forest into the world's tar and timber spine.
