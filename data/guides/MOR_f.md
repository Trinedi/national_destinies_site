## Concept

Morocco is the direct continuation of the Sharifian state tradition:
Idrisid, Almoravid, Almohad, Marinid, Wattasid, Saadi, and Alaouite
dynasties, all centred on the Fez-Meknes-Marrakesh axis, all anchored
in moroccan culture, and all defined by the dual orientation toward
the Mediterranean and the Atlantic. Forming MOR formalises the
Sharifian sultanate that historically reached its peak under Ahmad
al-Mansur after the Battle of the Three Kings (Alcacer Quibir, 1578)
and Moulay Ismail's Alaouite consolidation around Meknes.

In EU5 the Marinid tag (MOR) is already most of the way there at
start. The formable mostly exists as a narrative reset: the Marinid
collapse, the rise of the Saadi, and the Alaouite reunification all
happen within the formable's advance and destiny tree.

## Forming it

You must be moroccan culture and own 75 percent of two areas: Morocco
(Fez, Meknes, Marrakesh, Rabat, Sale, the Atlantic plain) and Sus
(the southern Atlantic coast, Agadir, Taroudant, Tiznit).

1. **Pick a Moroccan-cultured tag.** MOR for the orthodox start, SLE
   or TET for an underdog Atlantic or Mediterranean port start.
2. **Hold the Morocco and Sus areas.** MOR already owns most of this;
   SLE and TET start with one location each and must conquer the
   rest from MOR, FEZ, MRK, and the Sus tag.
3. **Form MOR.** No specific city is required; just the area
   fractions.

Note that FEZ is zenati culture and MRK is masmuda culture in vanilla
EU5, so neither can form Morocco directly. They model the Berber
political class displaced by the Arab-Andalusi Sharifian tradition.

## Pick your founding doctrine on formation

The formation event offers two 20-year doctrines:

- **The Sharif Commands the Faithful.** Sharifian descent from the
  Prophet as political legitimacy. Land army, religious authority,
  Maghreb-wide ambition. Unlocks Path A (Maghreb Secured -> Masters
  of the Golden Road -> Western Caliphate Proclaimed), the
  caliph-of-the-west path that ends in Caliphal Piety or Caliphal
  Sword.
- **Gateway of Two Seas.** Atlantic and Mediterranean dual
  orientation. Naval, commercial, port-development focus. Unlocks
  Path B (Strait Secured -> Atlantic Ventures -> Master of Two
  Oceans), the maritime path that ends in Oceanic Fleet or Trade
  Empire.

## What happens on formation

- Country tag becomes MOR.
- 20-year founding modifier from your event choice.
- The Marinid-Wattasid-Saadi-Alaouite chronology compresses into the
  advance tree, with DHE events for the Battle of the Three Kings
  (Alcacer Quibir) and Moulay Ismail's reign at Meknes.

## After forming

Two destiny paths, gated by your formation choice. Path A pushes for
trans-Saharan reach (the historical Saadi conquest of Songhai in
1591) and a western caliphate; Path B pushes for the Strait,
Atlantic outposts, and a port-network trade empire competing with
the Iberians.

## Notes

- The vanilla EU5 `MAR` tag is Maryul in the Tibetan plateau, not
  Marinid Morocco. The Marinid sultanate is `MOR` in EU5, and is
  already the canonical Moroccan starter.
- The formation event includes 18th century Alaouite flavour
  (Moulay Ismail) projected back; treat the advance tree as a
  compressed Sharifian chronology, not a linear 1337 narrative.
