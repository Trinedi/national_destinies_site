## Concept

The Two Sicilies is the post-Norman southern Italian crown. Roger II
unified Naples and Sicily in 1130; the Vespers of 1282 split them
again into an Angevin mainland and an Aragonese island, and the EU5
start date catches the kingdom mid-fracture. Forming TWS reverses the
Vespers, fusing neapolitan and sicilian populations under one throne.

The historical reference is the Bourbon Two Sicilies of 1816, but the
flavour reaches back through Hohenstaufen Sicily under Frederick II
(stupor mundi), Norman Palermo's Greek-Arab-Latin court, and the
Mezzogiorno's role as the Mediterranean's granary, supplying Genoa,
Venice, and the Levant with hard wheat through medieval famines.

## Forming it

You must be neapolitan or sicilian culture, must own Naples and
Palermo, and must control 80 percent of the area pool: Basilicata,
Apulia, Abruzzo, Campania, and Sicily. Italy (ITA) cannot form it. The
trigger explicitly handles the rivalry: if you are SIC, NAP must not
hold any Italian land; if you are NAP, SIC must not hold any Italian
land.

1. **Pick NAP or SIC.** Both are the natural starters; the run is the
   same shape from either side.
2. **Eliminate the rival.** Take Naples from NAP or Palermo from SIC,
   depending on who you are. The trigger requires the rival to have no
   presence in Italy, so peace deals must strip them out fully or
   annex them.
3. **Hold all five areas.** Basilicata, Apulia, Abruzzo, Campania,
   Sicily. The 80 percent fraction is tight; subjugating Aragonese,
   Angevin, or Papal holdings on the mainland is usually mandatory.
4. **Form TWS.** Capital and culture remain whichever side you started
   on. The formation event grants 20 years of either Crown of the
   Mezzogiorno (centralising) or Crossroads of the Mediterranean
   (mixed-trade, tolerance-leaning).

## Pick your founding doctrine on formation

The formation event offers two 20-year doctrines:

- **Crown of the Mezzogiorno.** Bourbon-style centralisation. Suppress
  the barons, impose royal law, build a modern fiscal state. Unlocks
  Path A (Crown of the South -> Master of the Peninsula -> Rex Italiae)
  which pushes for an Italian crown.
- **Crossroads of the Mediterranean.** The Norman-Hauteville model:
  Greek, Arab, Norman, and Latin traditions coexisting, foreign
  merchants welcomed in every port. Unlocks Path B (Mare Nostrum ->
  Ionian Trade Routes -> Emporium of the Mediterranean) which pushes
  for thalassocracy.

## What happens on formation

- Country tag becomes TWS.
- 20-year founding modifier from your event choice.
- The rival's mainland presence has already been removed by the
  `allow` trigger before the form effect fires.

## After forming

Two destiny paths, gated by your formation choice:

- **Path A: Rex Italiae.** Crown of the South -> Master of the
  Peninsula (choice between Iron Crown and Roman Legacy modifiers) ->
  Rex Italiae (Stupor Mundi or Mezzogiorno Ascendant). The path of
  Frederick II: a southern-anchored Italian kingdom that contests Rome
  and the north.
- **Path B: Mediterranean Empire.** Mare Nostrum -> Ionian Trade
  Routes (Adriatic Dominion or Sea-Silk-Road modifiers) -> Emporium of
  the Mediterranean (Thalassocracy or Emporium Mundi). The path of
  Norman Sicily and Bourbon Naples as a maritime power, with fondaci
  in every Levantine port.

## Notes

- Vanilla EU5 puts a Knights of Malta tag in Italy under a different
  identifier; the EU5 `MLT` tag is Malacato in Ecuador, so the
  Hospitallers cannot form Two Sicilies.
- The formation event includes Bourbon-era flavour (Bourbon Reforms
  DHE) even though the start date is 1337; treat the advance tree as
  a compressed chronology of the southern crown's whole history.
