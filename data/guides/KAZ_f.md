## Concept

The Kazakh Khanate did not exist in 1337. Historically it was founded
around 1465 when Janibek and Kerei led roughly two hundred thousand
nomads away from Abu'l-Khayr's Uzbek confederation onto the open
steppe of the Desht-i Kipchak. The mod frames the formation as that
exodus moment: a Chinggisid-rooted nomadic state whose central
question is whether to centralise as one khanate (the legacy of Kasym
Khan) or institutionalise the three-zhuz confederal balance.

## Forming it

The vanilla gate is generous: Kipchak-language culture and 75 percent
of two areas (`desht_kipchak_area` and `zhetysu_area`). No religion
gate, no rank gate, no allow block. The challenge is owning the
territory, not unlocking the formable.

1. **Start as a Kipchak-language tag.** GLH, CHG, BSH, or smaller
   Tartar-group tags. Cultures in this language family include kazani,
   uzbek_culture, cuman_culture, nogai, bashkir, mishar, astrakhani,
   crimean, and kumyk_culture.
2. **Hold 75 percent of the steppe core.** Desht-i Kipchak runs from
   the lower Volga across the Aral steppe to the Irgiz; Zhetysu is the
   Seven Rivers country between Lake Balkhash and the Tian Shan
   piedmont (Almaty, Talas, Balasagun, Kayalyk).
3. **Form Kazakh.** No further conditions.

## Pick your founding doctrine on formation

The formation event offers two 15-year doctrines:

- **Legacy of Kasym.** One khan, one nation, the centralised
  Chinggisid model. Unlocks Path A (Jeti Zhargy legal code, expansion,
  centralised steppe state).
- **Three Zhuz Confederacy.** Confederal balance under the biys and
  sultans. Unlocks Path B (zhuz autonomy, trade and clan diplomacy).

## What happens on formation

- A 15-year formation modifier from your chosen path.
- Mild stability bonus.
- The destiny tree unlocks, with the Jeti Zhargy advance event tied
  to Tauke Khan's seventeenth-century legal codification as a later
  callback regardless of path.

## Notes

- The mod's destiny tree exists but the mod's events file beyond
  the formation and Jeti Zhargy advance events was not read in full
  for this guide; the in-game tree itself is the canonical reference
  for path-specific stages.
