## Concept

The Durrani Empire revives Ahmad Shah Abdali's 1747 to 1823 Pashtun
empire, the last great Indo-Persian polity before the British and Sikh
ascendancy. The historical reference is the Loya Jirga of Kandahar that
crowned Ahmad Shah Pearl of Pearls (Durr-i-Durran), and the nine
invasions that carried Afghan armies from Khorasan to Delhi, peaking
at the Third Battle of Panipat in 1761.

The mod's framing is a confederal mountain empire built on Pashtunwali,
the jirga, Sufi networks, and the Khyber and Bolan toll regime. It sits
at the Persia / Central Asia / India crossroads with destiny branches
that either consolidate Khorasan as the heartland or push east to rule
the Indus and the Yamuna.

## Forming it

The formation gates are unusual: your country must be **afghan_culture**
and **Sunni**, but no tag at game start has afghan_culture as primary.
The Pashtun culture flip is part of the run, not something you can shop
for at game start.

1. **Pick a starter.** NGD (Kandahar) and QUN (Kabul) are the geographic
   cleanest choices but start as steppe hordes with shafii_school. SWT
   in the Pashtun belt and MIH in Sistan are Sunni monarchies with
   afghan_culture already tolerated; both need a longer march.
2. **Hold both required locations.** The `allow` block requires you to
   own **Kandahar** and **Kabul**. NGD starts with Kandahar; QUN starts
   with Kabul. You must take the other.
3. **Convert primary culture to afghan_culture.** afghan_culture is
   tolerated by NGD, QUN, SWT, MIH, KHN, and BSD. Promote it through
   pop conversion or estate-driven flips.
4. **Be Sunni.** NGD, QUN, SWT, MIH all start Sunni. If you start
   anything else, convert.
5. **Hold half of the wider area pool.** Beyond Kandahar and Kabul you
   need 50 percent of Sistan, Khorasan east, Khorasan west, Baluchistan,
   Kerman, Quhestan, Upper Indus, Punjab, and Sindh combined.

## Pick your founding doctrine on formation

The formation event offers two 30-year doctrines:

- **Loya Jirga of Kandahar.** The 1747 constitutional moment, the
  tribal council that crowned Ahmad Shah. Gameplay focus: confederal
  legitimacy, subject loyalty, internal cohesion. Pulls society toward
  decentralization. Unlocks the Khorasan Heartland destiny path.
- **Sword of Khorasan.** The Sadozai war machine: ghulam khana, zamburak
  camel artillery, the army that won Panipat. Gameplay focus: military
  power, mobility, conquest. Pulls society toward belligerence. Unlocks
  the Indian Empire destiny path.

## What happens on formation

- Capital moves to Kandahar.
- Country rank rises to Empire if below it.
- Mild stability bonus.
- Formation event fires with the two doctrine choices.

## After forming

The destiny tree splits two ways:

- **Khorasan Heartland.** Refuse the Indian temptation; rule from the
  mountain plateau. Required conquests reach Mashhad and Nishapur, then
  Merv and Bukhara, then Samarkand and Isfahan. Capstone building is
  the Qanat Network, irrigation infrastructure for Persianate Khorasan.
  Unlocked by the Loya Jirga formation choice.
- **Sword of Khorasan / Indian Empire.** Continue Ahmad Shah's nine
  invasions. Required conquests reach Lahore and Multan, then Delhi,
  Agra, and Sirhind, then Srinagar, Thatta, and Peshawar. Capstone
  building is the Imperial Diwan, a Mughal-style imperial court at the
  Bala Hissar of Kabul. Unlocked by the Sword of Khorasan formation
  choice.

## Notes

- The advance tree leans heavily on Pashtun identity: Pashtunwali, the
  jirga, Sufi networks, jezail rifles, zamburak camel artillery, and
  the Panipat doctrine. Several advances trigger DHE flavor events.
- The formation requirement of afghan_culture as primary is the real
  gate. Plan the culture flip well before you have the locations.
