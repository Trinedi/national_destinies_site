## Concept

The Muisca Confederation unifies the Altiplano Cundiboyacense, the
high plateau around modern Bogota and Tunja, under a single state.
The historical reference is the pre-Columbian Muisca polity that
the Spanish encountered in 1537: the Zipa ruling from Bacata
(modern Bogota) over the southern half, the Zaque ruling from Hunza
(modern Tunja) over the northern half, with the Iraca priest-king at
Sugamuxi presiding over religion. Tundama at Duitama led the most
prominent resistance against Gonzalo Jimenez de Quesada's conquest.

The Muisca are also the source of the El Dorado myth: the gilded man
ceremony at Lake Guatavita, where a new Zipa was covered in gold dust
and rowed onto the lake to make offerings, was filtered back to
Spanish chroniclers as a city of gold somewhere in the South American
interior. The mod's formation event makes the choice explicit:
inherit El Dorado's wealth tradition, or inherit Tundama's defiance.

## Forming it

1. **Start as a muisca-cultured tag** (ZIP or ZAQ). The potential
   gate is `culture = culture:muisca_culture`. Only these two tags
   qualify in 1337.
2. **Own funza and chunsua.** ZIP starts with funza, ZAQ starts with
   chunsua. Whichever you pick, you must take the other capital. The
   two tags border each other on the Altiplano, so this is a single
   short war.
3. **Hold 65 percent of Chunsa and Boiaca.** The required-locations
   fraction is 0.65 across the Chunsa (Cundinamarca) and Boiaca
   (Boyaca) areas. The two starting tags between them already cover
   most of this; you only need to mop up the highland gaps.
4. **Form the Confederation.** This is `rule = historical`, the
   tightest plausibility class, so the formable appears only when
   the historical Zipa-Zaque union conditions are clearly met.

## Pick your founding doctrine on formation

The formation event offers two 20-year doctrines:

- **Path of the Golden Zipa.** Inherit the El Dorado tradition.
  Trade efficiency, production efficiency, and cultural focus on the
  goldsmith and emerald trade networks that made the Muisca the
  envy of pre-Columbian South America. Path A, El Dorado's Legacy,
  requires this choice.
- **Tundama's Defiance.** Inherit the guecha warrior tradition.
  Land morale, hostile attrition, discipline, and manpower. The
  highlands as an impregnable bastion. Path B, The Highland Fortress,
  requires this choice.

## What happens on formation

- Capital moves to **funza** (Bacata).
- Permanent **Zipa-Zaque Compact** country modifier is granted (the
  dual kingship governance with the Iraca priest-king at Sugamuxi).
- Mild stability bonus.
- Formation event fires with the two doctrine choices.

## After forming

The destiny tree splits two ways.

- **El Dorado's Legacy.** The trade-and-gold path. Stage 1 (Emerald
  Roads) takes caly and mompoj plus 1000 gold. Stage 2 (Markets of
  Gold) demands quito, the Banking institution, and 2000 gold. Stage
  3 (Golden Kingdom) requires panama, 1500 development, and 75
  prestige. Capstone is the Guatavita Treasury. Unlocked by the
  Path of the Golden Zipa formation choice.
- **The Highland Fortress.** The guecha-defensive path. Stage 1
  (Tairona War) takes teyuna and zenues with a 30-army gate. Stage 2
  (Mountain Dominion) demands caracas and coro with a 50-army gate.
  Stage 3 (Andean Hegemony) requires qusqu (Cusco), 1500 development,
  and 75 prestige. Capstone is the Tundama Citadel. Unlocked by the
  Tundama's Defiance formation choice.

The path you cannot take with a given doctrine still appears in the
tree but its first stage requires the other path's variable. Commit
on formation.

## Notes

- DHE chain follows the historical iconography: El Dorado ceremony
  at Guatavita (Age 2), Tundama's Valor (Age 2 vanguard unit),
  Sugamuxi tradition (Age 1 Temple of the Sun unlock).
- The advance tree includes guecha warriors and levies (the elite
  Muisca infantry tradition), salt and emerald trade buildings, the
  cotton manta weaving house, the Cercado palace for cacique
  councils, and a goldsmith workshop.
- ZIP and ZAQ are mirror starts. The decision is mostly thematic:
  do you want to enter the formation as the El Dorado southern Zipa,
  or as the more militant northern Zaque tradition? The mechanics
  converge.
