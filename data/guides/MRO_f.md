## Concept

The Moro Sultanate is a pan-Mindanao Sunni Islamic confederation that
unifies the historical Sulu, Maguindanao, Lanao, Iranun, Tagakaulo,
and Butuanon polities into a single sultanate. The reference points
are Sharif ul-Hashim's founding sermons in Sulu, Sultan Kudarat's
17th-century defiance of the Spanish, the lantaka swivel-gun and vinta
lateen fleets that made the Moros the masters of the inner seas, and
the long resistance of the juramentado kris-warrior tradition.

The mod transcends the vanilla Sulu / Maguindanao / Lanao / Kiliman
silos: the formable is the unified Moro polity, the political project
that the historical sultanates pursued but never fully achieved.

## Forming it

1. **Start as a Moro-cultured tag.** Eligible cultures are Tausug
   (SUL), Maguindanao (MGD), Iranun (LNO), Tagakaulo (KIM), and
   Butuanon (BTU). No other primary culture qualifies.
2. **Be Sunni.** SUL and BTU start hindu; MGD, KIM, and LNO start
   anitist. All five candidates need a religion flip to Sunni. This
   is the dominant gating step. The historical conversion through
   Sharif ul-Hashim's mission to Sulu is the obvious narrative anchor.
3. **Hold the two named locations.** The `allow` block requires you
   to own **Maimbung** (Sulu, held by SUL) and **Kuta Watu**
   (Maguindanao, held by MGD).
4. **Hold two of the four secondary anchors.** You must own at least
   two of **Butuan** (BTU), **Malabang** (LNO), **Buayan** (KIM), and
   **Samboangan** (SML, the Sanmalan tausug-tolerated tag holding the
   Zamboanga peninsula).
5. **Hit 80 percent of Mindanao.** The breadth requirement is most
   of the Mindanao area pool.
6. **Not in civil war.** The form effect blocks if you have an active
   civil war.

## Pick your founding doctrine on formation

The formation event offers three 20-year doctrines:

- **Sultan and Caliph.** The sultan as Caliph of the Southern Seas, an
  Islamic monarchy modelled on Mecca and Aceh. Gameplay focus: religious
  authority, centralization, jurisprudence. Pulls society toward
  centralization and jurisprudence. Unlocks the Sultan and Pandita
  destiny path (sharia codification, pan-Islamic trade, madrasah
  civilization).
- **Datu Confederation.** The federation of datus as the political
  spine, the sultan as first among equals. Gameplay focus: noble
  legitimacy, decentralized administration. Pulls society toward
  decentralization and aristocracy. **No destiny continuation.** Pick
  this only if you want the founding modifier without committing to
  either destiny path.
- **Sword and Sail.** The vinta and lantaka path: corsair fleets,
  juramentado warrior corps, naval mastery. Gameplay focus: navy,
  mobility, raid economy. Pulls society toward naval and belligerent.
  Unlocks the Sword of the Caliph destiny path (juramentado corps,
  lantaka armada, Caliphate of the Southern Seas).

## What happens on formation

- Country rank rises to Kingdom if below it.
- Mild stability bonus.
- Formation event fires with the three doctrine choices.

## After forming

The destiny tree splits two ways:

- **Sultan and Pandita** (Path B in the events). Codify sharia under
  the qadi, build a pan-Islamic trade network from Aceh to Hajj
  caravans, crown the realm as a madrasah civilization with tariqa
  brotherhoods and a Sultan Solomon legacy. Unlocked by the Sultan and
  Caliph formation choice.
- **Sword of the Caliph** (Path A in the events). Train kris brotherhoods
  and corsair-juramentado boarding parties, master the inner seas with
  the lantaka armada, and proclaim the Caliphate of the Southern Seas
  with a corsair empire holding Ternate, Api Api, and Sandakan.
  Unlocked by the Sword and Sail formation choice.

## Notes

- The Datu Confederation founding choice is a deliberate dead-end. It
  is the historically loyal option (the datus did rule as a confederation
  in practice) but the destiny tree only continues from the two more
  centralized founding visions.
- Several advances fire DHE flavor events: Sharif ul-Hashim, vinta and
  lantaka, Sultan Kudarat, the pandita schools, and the juramentado
  tradition.
