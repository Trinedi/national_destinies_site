## Concept

The Songhai Empire is the great Sahelian successor to Mali: Sunni Ali
the river-warrior who broke Tuareg and Mossi alike, and Askia Muhammad
the pilgrim-emperor who turned conquest into a civilisation of Islamic
law and Timbuktu scholarship. Forming it sets your capital to Gao,
pushes you to empire rank, and applies a stability bump.

The two paths split this directly: **Sunni Ali's river conqueror**
versus **Askia's scholar emperor**.

## Forming it

1. **Start as a Songhai tag** (SON or TFK). Both are Sunni Maliki
   already, no religion flip needed.
2. **Hold Gao and Timbuktu.** SON owns Gao but not Timbuktu; Timbuktu
   belongs to Mali (MAL) at start and must be taken by war or
   diplomatic absorption.
3. **Hit 55% of a six-area pool.** Gao, Timbuktu, Mali, Zarma, Air, and
   Ghana. The fraction is modest but the pool spans the whole western
   Sahel, so you will be fighting Mali, Tuareg, and Hausa neighbours.
4. **Pick your path on formation.**
   - *Riders of the Niger*: Sunni Ali's river-cavalry doctrine,
     military emphasis.
   - *Throne of knowledge and justice*: Askia's pilgrimage and
     Timbuktu's madrasas, administrative and religious emphasis.

## Pick your founding doctrine on formation

The formation event offers two 30-year doctrines. Picking one also gates
which destiny path you can later take.

- **Riders of the Niger** (*River Conqueror*, 30 years). Sunni Ali's
  doctrine: noble cavalry in iron breastplates and the Hi-Koy war
  canoes ruling the Niger. Stat focus: light and heavy cavalry power,
  army movement speed, manpower, land morale, hostile attrition,
  reduced war exhaustion. Gates the Dominion of the Niger destiny path
  (path A, Sahel Hegemony line).
- **Throne of knowledge and justice** (*Scholar Emperor*, 30 years).
  Askia Muhammad's doctrine: the Hajj caliphate, Sankore madrasas,
  Maliki jurisprudence, and standardized weights. Stat focus: research
  speed, institution growth, tolerance, diplomatic reputation,
  literacy, stability cost reduction. Gates the Light of Timbuktu
  destiny path (path B, Beacon of Africa line).

## What happens on formation

- Capital is force-set to Gao.
- Country rank rises to Empire if currently below rank 4.
- Mild stability bonus.
- Formation event fires with the two doctrine choices.

## After forming

The destiny tree splits two ways:

- **Path A: Dominion of the Niger.** Conquest line. Stage 1 secures
  Taghaza salt and Ouagadougou. Stage 2 takes Kano, Katsina, and
  Ngazargamu, conquering Hausaland and Bornu. Stage 3 reaches Kumasi,
  Bono Manso, and Sokoto for total Sahel hegemony, and unlocks the
  **Imperial War Camp** (`nd_soe_imperial_warcamp`) capstone building.
  Unlocked by the Riders of the Niger formation choice.
- **Path B: Light of Timbuktu.** Scholarly line. Stage 1 takes Begho
  and secures gold market access. Stage 2 embraces banking at 2500
  total development. Stage 3 embraces the printing press at 3500
  development and unlocks the **House of Wisdom**
  (`nd_soe_house_of_wisdom`) capstone building. Unlocked by the Throne
  of knowledge and justice formation choice.

## Notes

- Capital is force-set to Gao on formation.
- Rank is set to empire if you are below rank 4.
- The 30-year formation modifier makes early formation very valuable.
- Later advance events fire on Askia's pilgrimage, the Battle of Tondibi
  collapse, and Timbuktu's scholarly golden age.
