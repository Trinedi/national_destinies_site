## Concept

Aotearoa, the United Tribes of New Zealand, takes its name and its
formation gate from He Whakaputanga o te Rangatiratanga o Nu Tireni,
the 1835 Declaration of Independence signed by the northern rangatira
that asserted Maori sovereignty as a single political entity. The mod
treats this as the unification moment: the iwi of the islands set
aside generations of inter-tribal warfare to constitute Aotearoa as
one rangatiratanga, choosing whether to root that unity in Tu (the
warrior tradition of haka, taiaha, and the toa) or Tangaroa (the
oceanic mastery of the ancestral wayfinders).

## Forming it

The vanilla gate is two conditions:

1. **Maori primary culture.** All starting NZ iwi are Maori-cultured
   pop tags following tapu_religion: PUH (Ngapuhi), TNI (Waikato
   Tainui), ARW (Te Arawa), NPO (Ngati Porou), NKH (Ngati Kahungunu),
   NTH (Ngai Tuhoe), HWE, NGI, NGR, RGT, AWA, RUA, and others.
2. **Hold the He Whakaputanga advance** (`maori_he_whakaputanga_advance`).
   This is an age 1 culture-locked advance requiring legalism, with
   a subject loyalty bonus, available to any Maori-cultured tag.
3. **Hold 50 percent of the New Zealand region.** Most iwi start with
   only one or two locations, so a unification campaign is required.

The starting iwi are tribal `type = pop` entities in 1337. The
formable is the historical-anachronistic culmination, not a 1337
starting state.

## Pick your founding doctrine on formation

The formation event offers two 20-year doctrines:

- **Children of Tu.** Strength through battle. The warrior tradition,
  haka, taiaha, the unbreakable will of the toa. Unlocks Path A (mana
  o te toa, the martial path).
- **Children of Tangaroa.** Mastery of the sea. Polynesian
  navigational heritage, oceanic trade and projection. Unlocks Path B
  (mana o te moana, the maritime path).

## What happens on formation

- A 20-year formation modifier from your chosen path.
- Mild stability bonus.
- The Musket Wars advance event becomes available later in the run
  (a callback to the 1807-1845 conflicts that reshaped Maori
  political geography around Pakeha trade).

## Notes

- The 50 percent NZ-region fraction is the hard task. Plan to absorb
  or vassalise three or four neighbouring iwi before the He
  Whakaputanga advance is even worth taking.
- Because every plausible starter is a Maori-cultured pop tag in the
  same region, the choice of starter is mainly geographic: northern
  push (PUH), central interior (TNI), or east coast (NKH).
