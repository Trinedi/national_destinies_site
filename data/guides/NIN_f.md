## Concept

The Assyrian Empire (tag NIN, after Nineveh, since vanilla EU5 already
uses ASR for Astarabad in Persia) is the Tier 2 endgame of the Syriac
restoration chain. It represents the imperial peak of the Neo-Assyrian
empire under Esarhaddon and Ashurbanipal, channeled through medieval
Syriac Christian institutions.

The historical reference is the 671 BC Sargonid peak: direct rule over
northern Mesopotamia, Babylonia, Elam, the Levant, Cyprus, and Egypt
itself (Memphis taken 671 BC, Thebes sacked 663 BC), with tributary
reach across Anatolia and western Iran. Roughly 1.4 million km square
of direct control plus another 1 million km square in tributaries.

NIN's medieval Syriac framing makes the Patriarchate of the East the
political nucleus of the imperial revival. The Catholicos at Seleucia
crowns the Assyrian emperor at the rebuilt Akitu altar. Aramaic civil
service, Bar Hebraeus library scholarship, and Tur Abdin monastic
defense from the Tier 1 BNH stage are now the foundation institutions
of a Sargonid-tier empire.

## Why no auto-recommendation

The formation gate is unusual. It requires miaphysite or nestorianism
religion AND one of: had-tag BNH, Kingdom rank, or Empire rank. The
auto-derived list of miaphysite countries that meet a Kingdom-rank
filter is short and dominated by Ethiopian Empire successors who are
geographically too distant to be a realistic path. The genuinely
viable starters are BNH (after the Tier 1 chain step) and Cilicia,
both of which need calling out by hand.

## Forming it

There are two paths.

### Path 1: The chain (BNH first, then NIN)

1. **Form BNH** following the BNH guide. Pick a small Caucasian Armenian
   principality or ATZ, conquer Mosul, Nusaybin, and Urfa, and form Tier 1.
2. **Choose Tur Abdin Defenders or Patriarch's Restoration** at BNH
   formation. Both unlock destiny paths. Tur Abdin Defenders flows into
   "Restoration of Aššur" Path A which signposts directly toward NIN.
3. **Build out BNH's heritage tree.** Kisir-style standing army
   prerequisites, riverine logistics, monastic recruitment, qanat
   infrastructure. Stack BNH's Path A destiny as you push imperial.
4. **Conquer the imperial peak.** The five required locations are
   Mosul, Baghdad, Damascus, Cairo, and either Shush (ancient Susa,
   the eastern frontier per Ashurbanipal) or Tarsus (the Cilician
   frontier per Sargon II). Plus 60 percent of Crescent and Egypt
   regions and the Khuzestan and Cilicia areas.
5. **Form NIN.** Country rank rises to Empire. Capital is set to
   Mosul. Stability gets a severe bonus. The formation event fires.

### Path 2: Cilicia direct (skip BNH)

1. **Start as Cilicia.** Already miaphysite, already at Kingdom rank,
   already adjacent to the Edessa frontier.
2. **Skip BNH.** Cilicia is too high rank for Tier 1, so go straight
   for the L4 conquest.
3. **Take all five required locations** (Mosul, Baghdad, Damascus,
   Cairo, plus Shush or Tarsus) and the 60 percent fraction.
4. **Form NIN.** Cilicia's primary culture is Armenian; on formation
   the form effect changes country culture to Syriac and converts
   Armenian, Iraqi, Kurdish, Levantine, Turkish, Farsi, Coptic, and
   Egyptian state pops to Syriac. Country rank rises to Empire.

The chain path gives you twenty-five years of BNH's Tier 1 institutions
to draw on, plus a fully built-out heritage tree before you start the
imperial conquest. The Cilicia direct path is faster and avoids the
Tier 1 detour, but you start the imperial campaign without the BNH
investments.

## Pick your founding doctrine on formation

The formation event offers three 40-year doctrines:

- **Sargonid Imperium.** Military-conquest doctrine. Channel the
  Neo-Assyrian war machine: kisir sharruti standing army,
  qurubuti royal guard, siege engineering. Stat focus: morale,
  discipline, siege ability, manpower, attrition reduction.
- **Pax Assyriaca.** Administrative-imperial doctrine. The Sargonid
  provincial system, Aramaic civil service, deportation and integration
  practice as a tool of empire. Stat focus: cabinet efficiency,
  control, assimilation speed, integration speed, infrastructure.
- **The Cross and the Lamassu.** Civilizational synthesis doctrine.
  Patriarch and emperor in one ritual, ancient Assyrian symbolism
  reframed Christian. Stat focus: tolerance, cultural influence,
  prestige, missionary networks.

## Destiny

Two paths after formation:

- **Path A: Pax Sargonica.** Push beyond the historical Neo-Assyrian
  peak. Required conquests reach Anatolia (Konya, Kayseri, Ankara,
  Smyrna), deeper Iran (Hamadan, Istakhr), and the Achaemenid east
  (Samarkand, Peshawar). Capstone building is **Bīt-Mātāti**, the Hall
  of the Lands, a universal-empire throne hall. Maps from the Sargonid
  Imperium formation choice.
- **Path B: New Mesopotamia.** Civilizational hegemon. Empire-wide Beth
  Hekma libraries, Aramaic as a global lingua franca, Christian
  missionary networks from Iberia to China, Tigris-Euphrates riverine
  infrastructure as the world's economic spine. Capstone building is
  the **Imperial Canal of Sennacherib**. Maps from The Cross and the
  Lamassu formation choice.

The Pax Assyriaca formation choice has no destiny continuation by
design. Pick it only if you want the formation modifier without
committing to either destiny path. This follows the same convention
as HIN, IRA, JAP, and USA, where one of three formation options is a
flavor doctrine without a destiny tree.

## What happens on formation

- **Capital moves to Mosul (Nineveh).**
- **Country culture flips to Syriac** for non-Syriac formers.
  State pops of every plausible Eastern conquered culture
  (Armenian, Iraqi, Kurdish, Levantine, Turkish, Farsi, Coptic, both
  Egyptian variants) convert to Syriac.
- **Country rank set to Empire** if below it.
- **Severe stability bonus.**
- **Formation event fires** with the three doctrine choices.

## Notes

- This is the Tier 2 capstone of the first intentional formable to
  formable chain in the mod. Most chains in the game (BYZ to ROM, JAP
  to YMP, MAM to AEG) connect a starting tag to a single formable.
  BNH to NIN connects two formables, with BNH itself being a Tier 1
  that has to be earned from a smaller starting tag.
- Custom content includes Lamassu Gate, Imperial Chancery, Riverine
  Naval Yard, Akitu Festival Square, Kisir Sharruti Barracks, Library
  of Ashurbanipal, Khorsabad Palace, plus Kisir Sharruti standing
  infantry, Qurubuti royal cavalry, Imperial Siege Corps, and Aramean
  Auxiliary levy.
- NIN heritage advances deliberately do not duplicate BNH themes.
  BNH covers patriarchate, monasticism, Bar Hebraeus scholarship,
  qanats, and Mosul-Baghdad caravans. NIN adds the Sargonid imperial
  layer on top: standing army, deportation administration, Khorsabad
  monumentalism, Library of Ashurbanipal, Aramaic civil service at
  imperial scale, Akitu coronation ritual.
